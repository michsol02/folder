> Organisational Culture
Introducing the issue 


Questions to answer 

Please answer some carefully crafted questions we've devised to help you apply the basics of this fascinating set of issues to your organisation, and describe some of the things you do, and why. Our current questions are:

Q1. Have you used or heard the term ‘organisational culture’ or 'corporate culture' in your workplace?

Q2. Is your corporate or organisational culture discussed or considered regularly or occasionally in the business?

Q3. If you deal with customers or suppliers in your role, are you required or expected to do so in a certain way, if so how?

Q4. How are “things done around here” in your organisation?

Q5. What are your organisation's values?

Q6. Does your company have any kind of written code regarding ethics or values?

Q7. Can you give examples of when board members and/or senior leaders acted according to the defined values of the company, or when they did not?

Q8. Can you name the person (or people) in your organisation who has overall responsibility for culture?

Q9. Is your organisation taking any steps to nurture, develop and improve its culture?

Q10. Is creating the best possible workplace culture a key strategic goal for your business?

Q11. What steps, if any, does your company take in an attempt to monitor, measure or influence organisational culture?

Q12. Does your company provide adequate means for whistleblowing without fear of reprisal or negative impacts on employees’ career prospects?

Q13. Do you feel psychologically safe to make any kind of suggestion e.g. as to new product ideas, how to improve work practices, or to raise any concerns with team dynamics?

Q14. Is groupthink an issue in your workplace?

Q15. Is your workplace culture evident in any day-to-day practices, behaviours or routines you can think of?

Q16. Is the culture of other organisations considered when entering into business relationships such as supplier, partnership or investment relationships?
