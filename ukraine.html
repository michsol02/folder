SUPPORT FOR UKRAINE

This first section is the same as our post on LinkedIn, skip to Section 2 if this is where you arrived here from.

1. We're seeking to help businesses that want to hasten peace in Ukraine

We've got an idea to support businesses and employees to take actions designed to hasten peace, or mitigate disaster, in Ukraine. We think we can be useful to businesses that are keen to discover what they can do. We're certainly going to try....

If you can take a minute or two to share details of any current plans or intentions you may have to respond to the crisis, we'll do the rest, ultimately sharing the results of this collaboration. Within a short period of time we hope to be able to provide a large number of ideas for actions. That is, to co-create a great new resource, which will include many actions you may then wish to undertake.

Our hypothesis is that the greater knowledge that businesses possess in regards to all the different actions they could take to hasten peace (or mitigate disaster) in Ukraine, the greater the chance they will do something. And for those that were always going to do something, the more ideas to peruse and the greater the chances of uncovering opportunities to collaborate, then the chances are that they'll end up doing more than they initially planned or imagined.

So, firstly, please respond to the poll. Secondly, please share any actions you have taken, or are considering, and any things you could do with help on, either in the comments below or via DM to me if you prefer.

2. Further details of what we propose to do

In addition to voting in the poll, please tell us about anything your business has done, or has contemplated doing, vis a vis Ukraine.

It would be especially useful to hear about things you will or might do on the basis you can get some form of help or support to do it - in which case please also explain what help you could use.

Responsible 100 looks at the widest range of social, environmental and ethical challenges that companies face, including business support for / involvement with oppressive regimes (in this case Putin's Russia). We believe in the power of benchmarks to help coordinate action and raise up standards. Our old benchmark on this needs updating, but you can view what we have here.

We categorise business performance on the responsibility issues we explore as POOR, OKAY, GOOD or EXCELLENT ('POGE'). Currently, for oppressive regimes, POOR is essentially 'who cares about peace / human rights?', OKAY is 'firm does the odd useful thing OR seeks to change the oppressive state by working with it', GOOD is 'firm does a lot re avoidance/withdrawal', and EXCELLENT is 'firm does everything it possibly can'.

This is what we propose doing:

1.
Crowdsourcing lots of different ideas for - and examples of - useful actions businesses and employees can take, along with details of the help and support any of those ideas and actions may require 

2.
Doing further desk research to gather more ideas, e.g. these resources were quickly found:
B Corp's B-interdependent page
A Google doc from Emerge

3.
Organising all the examples we receive, or can find, possibly arranging the information into increasingly detailed and useful versions of the oppressive regimes benchmark in the POGE format

4.
Sharing the results publicly to allow more businesses and employees to discover more, and identify more ideas and opportunities to act

In essence, this is the Responsible 100 approach to all the issues we explore.

Our experience is that businesses often fail to give themselves credit for the positive things they do, they frequently fail to ask for help which may well be readily available, and they fail to share with and learn from other businesses and organisations. These are functions we seek to undertake as they offer great ways to raise everyone's knowledge and performance levels up.

So, to repeat the main ask: please describe anything you or your business is doing, or might attempt to do, vis a vis hastening peace in Ukraine.

Ideally in the comments section of the original LinkedIn post so others can see them. Or alternatively via the contact info / option on this website.
