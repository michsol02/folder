SUPPORT FOR UKRAINE

Please spend a few minutes to contribute to a collaborative effort to help businesses work out which actions they can take to hasten peace in Ukraine and alleviate the resulting humanitarian crisis.

1. A crowdsourced resource

The invasion of Ukraine presents the world with multiple horrors. Paul Polman ends an excellent recent blog by urging business to "do whatever is required of us to help restore stability, uphold basic human values and strengthen the cooperation on which all our futures depend".

The ask is a small and simple one: please share basic details of what your company has done - or may yet do - in responding to the crisis, and indicate any help you require to do more. That's it. We'll then seek to collate responses and create a useful resource which we'll make freely and publicly available.

It struck us that:

>> >>
Most companies and most employees want to take action in response to Putin's despicable invasion of Ukraine;

>> >>
While many lists of proposed actions do exist the great majority are geared towards help individuals might provide, rather than organisations, teams or colleagues;

>> >>
Some kind of check list or How To guide listing many examples of different types of business actions has strong appeal; and

>> >>
The greater knowledge that businesses possess in regards to all the different actions they could take, the greater the chances they will do something, or do even more than they'd planned to.

If you can contribute to this effort, we'd be delighted to share the results with you at the earliest opportunity.

2. Possible actions (last updated 8 March 2022)

Within the four categories of business responses below, we've identified 3-6 adoptable actions. Against these actions please indicate:

1. If it, or something like it, is something you have done (add any specific details if relevant)
2. If it isn't an action you've undertaken yet, whether it is possible that you could in the future
3. Whether you need help or support to undertake the action, and if so what (we'll try to coordinate)
4. Lastly, add anything else you have done, or might do, or know of, that is not (yet) listed

A.  Employee wellbeing and agency
+  Check in on your colleagues: what do they think and how are they feeling?
+  Acknowledgement from the top (e.g. chief exec or directors) there is a crisis in Ukraine and we're all affected in different ways
+  Create psychological safety for employees to speak up and share their feelings, worries etc.
+  Seek ideas and other inputs from employees to help shape the company response
+  Encourage discussion and invite employees to collaborate and engage
+  Appoint and task company administrators or contact persons or create employee committees to undertake and coordinate company actions  

B.  Donate money or other resources
+  Donate money or services to support those affected on the ground
+  Match fund employee donations
+  Allow employees to work on the company responses on company time
 
C.  Use your business voice
+  Communicate support and solidarity for those in peril
+  Communicate your commitment to playing whatever parts you can as an organisation
+  Change your business logo to add Ukraine colours, change or vary your websites and email signatures
+  Post on social media to rally support
+  Demand action from your government or government representative(s)

D.  Develop and use your economic influence 
+  Discontinue business in Russia  
+  Terminate contracts to trade with the Russian state, Russian entities, to transport goods to or from Russia, to sell or operate there, or promote or sponsor
+  Squeeze the Russian economy to create internal pressure on Putin's regime through any other available means
+  Create new business with Ukrainians
+  Create or adopt codes to formulate company policy, guide future decisions and embed values
+  Support frameworks or multilateral efforts to deter other oppressive, coercive or oppressive regimes which disregard human rights and norms

Simply copy and paste the above list, and type in any response under relevant actions, and send that by email to info@responsible100.com.
