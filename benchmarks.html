BENCHMARKS

Benchmarking performance on a wide range of responsibility and sustainability issues, and using the results to inform and empower businesses, is at the heart of what we do.

Background

Responsible 100 grew out of an unease with standard approaches to corporate reporting on social, environmental and ethical issues. It is a good thing when businesses pursue appropriate policies and practices. As too is their desire to report on progress.

However, when gaps exist between their words and their actions, irrespective of whether those are real or imagined gaps, a myriad of problems occur. For one, public trust in business is diminished. Nowadays, a high proportion of the public simply refuse to believe that any business cares about being responsible at all. Distrust is a huge impediment to change.

We set out to enable businesses to report on their policies and practices in ways that were credible, believable and broadly accessible.

Our view was that genuinely responsible businesses would want to prove that they were sincere, and that they weren’t ‘greenwashing’.

Transparency and accountability across a wide range of issues was our initially proposed solution. Our ideas were intriguing to - and supported by - a wide range of organisations.

In developing our approach and creating benchmarks, we've collaborated with hundreds of businesses and supporter organisations. By including think tanks, NGOs, campaign groups, unions, trade bodies, government and academia, we've ensured the most relevant voices are being heard on each issue.

Our early experimentation led us to some unexpected discoveries. For example, as much as full transparency and accountability may help to demonstrate sincerity, it is incredibly difficult for practically all businesses to do. Frankly, it's scary to contemplate.

Further, we realised that engagement, discovery and assessment all appeared to be prerequisites for openness and honesty, and that undertaking this groundwork in itself offered huge benefits.

Disclosure & Discovery

We make it safe for businesses to reveal which policies and practices, if any, they pursue on any given issue.

No prior knowledge is required nor assumed. Issue by issue (these illustrations use Living Wage as an example), we explain the basics to participant businesses (e.g. the harm that ‘poverty wages’ cause, and how the Real Living Wage is designed to ensure the lowest paid earn enough to live on).

We provide prompts to help businesses make relevant, basic disclosures. These are the earliest steps in our process where nothing is shared nor published.

Collating Real Life Examples

We've explored thousands of different real-life policy and practice examples, issue by issue. And we’ve sought to understand and describe different standards of business response as either POOR, OKAY, GOOD or EXCELLENT. 

In so doing, we've built up an incredibly valuable, constantly evolving, open-sourced set of performance benchmarks. 

By benchmarking in this way, across their selected priority responsibility issues, we help businesses to discover the pathways open to them to achieve better social and environmental practices and outcomes.

The Power of Benchmarking

By working together in this way we socialise the problems that each issue presents, and we work collaboratively to explore and create possible solutions.

The further and deeper we've explored, the more we've discovered.

An incredible diversity of opinion, knowledge and experience is thus brought to bear in the appreciation of all the issues we examine.

We've created virtuous circles of learning and improvement.

Every time a business assesses against an issue and the associated Responsible 100 benchmark, our collective, shared knowledge and understanding is broadened.

As businesses discover and learn more, our whole community benefits.

Participants consider their current policies and practices in new lights. They describe what they do now. They describe which opportunities to change and improve that they intend to pursue.

With each use and iteration, the benchmarks are improved for the next participant. 

Collaborative exploration sparks our imaginations. It enables us to identify and prioritise ideas with the greatest potential to create real change. It inspires breakthroughs and innovation.  

Appreciating what really matters most and what makes the most difference is crucial. But so too is learning from instances when, despite best efforts, little or no difference has been made, or when a business has simply failed to find alternatives to POOR practices.
