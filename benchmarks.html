RESPONSIBLE 100 BENCHMARKS

Benchmarking performance on a wide range of responsibility and sustainability issues, and using the results to inform and empower businesses, is at the heart of what we do.

GET STARTED

Responsible 100 grew out of an unease with standard approaches to corporate reporting on social, environmental and ethical issues. It is a good thing when businesses pursue appropriate policies and practices. As too is their desire to report on progress.

However, when gaps exist between their words and their actions, irrespective of whether those are real or imagined gaps, a myriad of problems occur. For one, public trust in business is diminished. Nowadays, high proportions of the public simply refuse to believe that any business cares about being responsible at all. Distrust is a huge impediment to change.

We set out to enable businesses to report on their policies and practices in ways that were credible, believable and broadly accessible. Our view was that genuinely responsible businesses would want to prove that they were sincere, and that they weren’t ‘greenwashing’.

Transparency and accountability across a wide range of issues was our initially proposed solution. Our ideas were intriguing to - and supported by - a wide range of organisations.

In developing our approach and creating benchmarks, we've collaborated with hundreds of businesses and supporter organisations, including think tanks, NGOs, campaign groups, unions, trade bodies, government and academia.  

Our early experimentation led us to some unexpected discoveries. For example, as much as full transparency and accountability may help to demonstrate sincerity, it is incredibly difficult, and scary, for practically all businesses to do, or to even contemplate.

We also realised that engagement, discovery and assessment all appeared to be prerequisites for openness and honesty, and that this groundwork in itself offered huge benefits.

"Corporate reports are often regarded as greenwash because they fail to explore and reveal what really happens on the ground - Responsible 100 reflects reality "
Head of Corporate Responsibility & Ethics, Legal & General

In August 2020, BrewDog announced it was removing twice as much carbon from the air than it emits. Enabling the claim: “by drinking BrewDog beer, you're having a positive impact on the planet”. Great news for beer drinkers AND for the planet! In which other ways can businesses be EXCELLENT on climate?
